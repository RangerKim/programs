Outdoor Education Lesson Library

This index serves as a central hub for lesson plans, activities, and educational resources designed for outdoor educators, teachers, and rangers.

Available Lessons

Student-Facing Lessons

  • Endangered Species Lesson – Understanding threatened wildlife, extinction, and conservation efforts.
  • Animal Detectives: Adaptations & Habitats – Investigating animal adaptations and habitats found in the Floyds Fork parklands.
  • Electricity Explorers Lesson – Observing and experimenting with static and current electricity, conductors, insulators, and energy in everyday systems, connected to natural phenomena in Floyds Fork.
  • Ordovician Fossils & Ancient Seas – Exploring fossils from Floyds Fork, fossilization, preservation bias, and reconstructing ancient sea life through scientific illustration.

Ranger & Advanced Learner Lessons

  • Human Impacts on Energy Flow – Analyzing how energy moves through ecosystems and how human activities disrupt food webs, trophic relationships, and species survival.
